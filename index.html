About

Join the Team

Have you participated in previous Computing Club activities, and experienced first hand the great synergy running through the organisers, as well as with the participants? Do you see great experience, potential and passion in yourself, which can be put into use to further the cause of your fellow faculty-mates?

As students of SoC, we have reaped much benefits from the efforts of the previous Computing Club Management Committees. Heed the calling to contribute, to serve and to lead. Step up, present yourself to your fellow students, and take up the challenge as the NUS Students' Computing Club 20th Management Committee.

Did you know? The Management Committee is officially part of the SoC Student Leadership Programme. Joining the Management Committee accords you service hours under the programme.

What is happening right now?

The 20th MC General Elections Nominations have closed. Nominees may campaign for support during this period with campaign posters and personal social media posts. To put up posters in SoC, please come to our club room at COM1 02-14 for approval of posters. The official campaign speech sessions begin on Thursday, 24 August 2017, 7 PM.

Timeline

20th MC General Elections

This is an overview of the 20th MC General Elections process, along with important dates.

  • 0

    Extraordinary General Meeting (Constitutional Amendments)

    15 Aug 2017 (Wk 1 Tue)

    8 PM to 10 PM

    All Computing students are invited to the Computing Club EGM 1 to hear about the proposed changes to the Computing Club constitution and vote over the amendments!

    Please refer to our amended constitution draft (in red) here to view the exact changes.

  • 1

    Nominations Open for General Elections

    16 to 18 Aug 2017 (Wk 1 Wed to Fri)

    12 PM to 3 PM

    Nominations period is the time for Computing students to sign up and step up to be a nominee in the upcoming elections.

    The Nominations are open from 16 August 12pm till 18 August 6pm. You may submit your nomination forms by passing it to the Election Committee in the Student Lounge from 12pm to 3pm daily. You may also arrange alternative methods of submission (with valid reason) by contacting the Election Committee via email.

    Nominees are required to give speech and answer questions from the audience during the General Meeting. Nominees are also encouraged to campaign by engaging the students in school and putting up posters (while adhering to the rules) around the school area.

    You may submit your nominations (Form A & Form B) from 12 PM to 3 PM at our very own Student Lounge located at COM1 02-21.

    You can find the necessary documents for nominations here.

    Only full-time matriculated undergraduates of the School of Computing are eligible to stand for election, propose or second a candidate, and vote at the ballots.

  • 2

    20th MC Information Sessions

    16 and 17 Aug 2017 (Wk 1 Wed and Thu)

    11 AM to 1 PM, and 3 PM to 5 PM

    Not sure what it means to be in the MC? Come for our information session(s) to find out more!

    Designed to help club members understand what the Management Committee does over their term, the 19th Management Committee is scheduling two information sessions on the to share about their past experiences.

    We recommend interested club members to attend to get a better picture of the roles & responsibilities of an Elected Committee Member.

  • 3

    Meet The Advisors (MTA)

    21 and 22 Aug 2017 (Wk 2 Mon and Tue)

    9 AM to 6 PM (30-minute session)

    These sessions are for our staff advisors to get to know the nominees better.

    Nominees will be informed of their individual MTA timeslot via email.

  • 4

    General Elections Campaigning

    24 and 25 Aug 2017 (Wk 2 Thu and Fri)

    From 7 PM and 8.30 PM respectively

    Campaigning is the period nominated nominees are allowed to garner for votes in their favour for the upcoming voting period.

    Nominees will have to adhere to the rules as indicated on our Election Standing Orders, NUS Student Conduct as well as instructions from the Elections Committee.

  • 5

    General Elections Voting

    28 and 29 Aug 2017 (Wk 3 Mon and Tue)

    12 PM to 6 PM

    Nominees are elected into the 20th Management Committee only if at least two-thirds of their votes are in support of their candidacy. In the event of a contested vote, the nominee who receives the highest number of net yes votes gets elected to the position.

    Students may cast their votes in the Student Lounge from 12PM ~ 6PM.

    Please note that the Direct Elections system is no longer used. Nominees will run to enter the committee. The positions are to be determined in the internal elections consisting of both the 19th MC and the newly-elected 20th MC. All nominees must obtain the approval of no less than two-thirds of the valid votes in the internal elections.

    Physical paper voting will be held on our very own campus.

  • 6

    General Elections Voting Results

    29 Aug 2017 (Wk 3 Tue)

    10 PM

    Our Returning Officer, Ms Mindy Soh, will announce the voting results at about 10PM. Successful nominees will be elected into the 20th MC and enter the Internal Elections.

  • 7

    20th MC Internal Elections

    30 and 31 Aug 2017 *

    Time TBC

    The newly elected members of the MC will attempt to fill up the positions specified in the Constitution over the 2 days. More details will be released to the new members after the announcement of the voting results.

    * To be confirmed

  • 8

    19th Annual General Meeting

    21 Sep 2017 *

    The Annual General Meeting is an annual event where our outgoing Management Commmittee position holders will be sharing their review of the past year.

    * To be confirmed
