About

Join the Team

Have you participated in previous Computing Club activities, and experienced first hand the great synergy running through the organisers, as well as with the participants? Do you see great experience, potential and passion in yourself, which can be put into use to further the cause of your fellow faculty-mates?

As students of SoC, we have reaped much benefits from the efforts of the previous Computing Club Management Committees. Heed the calling to contribute, to serve and to lead. Step up, present yourself to your fellow students, and take up the challenge as the NUS Students' Computing Club 19th Management Committee.

Did you know? The Management Committee is officially part of the SoC Student Leadership Programme. Joining the Management Committee accords you service hours under the programme.

Running

19th MC Elections Timeline

This is an overview of the elections process, along with important dates.

  • 0

    Information Sessions

    12 - 2pm, 12 & 15 August 2016

    Designed to help club members understand what the Management Committee does over their term, the 18th Management Committee is scheduling two information sessions on the 12th and 15th of August, from 12noon to 2pm, to share about their past experiences.

    We recommend interested club members to attend to get a better picture of the roles & responsibilities of an Elected Committee Member.

  • 1

    Nominations

    10 - 17 August 2016

    Nominations period is the time for club members to sign up and step up to be a nominee in the upcoming elections.

    Successful nominees will be allowed to campaign and garner votes for the position they are running for.

    You may submit your nominations (Form A & Form B) from 12pm to 2pm or 4pm to 6pm at our very own Student Lounge located at COM1 02-21.

    You can find the necessary documents for nominations here

    Only full-time matriculated undergraduates of the School of Computing are eligible to stand for election, propose or second a candidate, and vote at the ballots.

  • 2

    Meet The Advisors (MTA)

    18 - 24 August 2016

    These sessions are for our staff advisors to get to know the nominees better.

    Nominees will be informed of their individual MTA timeslot via email.

  • 3A

    Extraordinary General Meeting I

    Thursday, 18 August 2016, 7 - 10pm

    The purpose of this General Meeting is to create the opportunity for nominees and the student body to engage in a two-way dialogue.

    The meeting may be streamed live, and participants (speakers) will be asked to wear formally with blazer.

    Agenda for meeting:

    1. Campaigning Speech(es) for Presidential Wing Nominees
    2. Campaigning Speech(es) for External Relations Wing Nominees

  • 3B

    Extraordinary General Meeting II

    Friday, 19 August 2016, 7 - 10pm

    The purpose of this General Meeting is to create the opportunity for nominees and the student body to engage in a two-way dialogue.

    The meeting may be streamed live, and participants (speakers) will be asked to wear formally with blazer.

    Agenda for meeting:

    1. Campaigning Speech(es) for Operations & Projects Wing Nominees
    2. Campaigning Speech(es) for Human Resource & Development Wing Nominees

  • 3C

    Campaigning

    18 - 24 August 2016

    Campaigning is the period nominated nominees are allowed to garner for votes in their favour for the upcoming voting period.

    Nominees will have to adhere to the rules as indicated on our Election Standing Order, NUS Student Conduct as well as instructions from the Elections Committee.

  • 4

    Voting

    25 - 31 August 2016

    The Direct Elections system is used. That is, nominees will run directly for a specific position. All nominees must obtain the approval of no less than 75% of the valid votes cast for the nominee. In the event of a contested vote, the nominee who receives the highest number of net yes votes gets elected to the position."

    Physical Paper Voting will be held on our very own campus.

    Timing & Location(s) will be disclosed in due time.

  • 5

    Results Annoucement

    Wednesday, 31 August 2016

    Upon announcement by our returning officer, the 19th Management Committee will be deemed elected by the student population.

  • 6

    Handover & Takeover

    1 Month from Announcement of Results

    The outgoing management committee will be handing over the assets, resources, contacts, connections, experiences and lessons to the incoming management committee.

  • 7

    18th Annual General Meeting

    Saturday, 17th September 2016*

    The outgoing management committee will be dissolved.
