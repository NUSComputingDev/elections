About

Join the Team

Have you participated in previous Computing Club activities, and experienced first hand the great synergy running through the organisers, as well as with the participants? Do you see great experience, potential and passion in yourself, which can be put into use to further the cause of your fellow faculty-mates?

As students of SoC, we have reaped much benefits from the efforts of the previous Computing Club Management Committees. Heed the calling to contribute, to serve and to lead. Step up, present yourself to your fellow students, and take up the challenge as the NUS Students' Computing Club 22nd Management Committee.

Timeline (TBC)

This is an overview of the 22nd MC General Elections process, along with the important dates.

  • 1

    22nd MC Information Sessions

    13 Aug and 14 Aug, 12:00PM to 04:00PM

    Foyer outside Cerebro@SoC

    This Information Session helps potential candidates understand what the Management Committee does. The 21st MC will be there to share their past experiences and answer any queries during the session.

  • 2

    21st Annual General Meeting

    14 Aug, 04:15PM to 07:00PM

    SR1

    The Annual General Meeting is an annual event where 21st Management Committee position holders will be sharing what they had done during their term. Students who are interested in running the 22nd Management Committee are strongly encouraged to attend the AGM to better understand the positions that they are running for.

  • 3

    Nomination Period

    13 Aug to 17 Aug, till 17 Aug 06:00PM

    Nominations period is the time for club members* to sign up and step up to be a nominee in the upcoming elections. Club members running for Computing Club MC should submit Form A and Form B, while club members running for NUSSU Exco should submit the NUSSU Exco Nomination Form. Club members are not allowed to run for both MC and NUSSU Exco. Forms can be found by clicking the button below.


    Download forms

    *Only full-time matriculated undergraduates of the School of Computing are eligible to stand for election, propose or second a candidate, and vote at the ballots.

  • 4

    Interview with advisors

    19 Aug, TBC

    Cerebro

    This session is for our faculty advisors to get to know the nominees better. Nominees will be informed of their individual interview details via email.

  • 5

    Appeal Period

    18 Aug to 19 Aug, till 19 Aug 11:59PM

    Any Club Member whose nomination is rejected by the Elections Committee shall have the right to a single appeal to the Elections Committee. The Elections Committee's decision following an appeal is final thereafter. The appeal must be submitted, in writing, to an Elections Committee member.

  • 6

    General Elections Campaigning Period

    18 Aug to 24 Aug, till 24 Aug 11:59PM

    Successful nominees will be allowed to campaign and garner votes for the position they are running for. Nominees are encouraged to put up posters on the noticeboard around School of Computing, and engage the students in school.

    Nominees will have to adhere to the rules as indicated on our Election Standing Order, NUS Student Conduct as well as instructions from the Elections Committee.

  • 7

    22nd MC Elections Campaign Speech

    21 Aug, 06:00PM to 10:00PM

    22 Aug, 06:00PM to 10:00PM

    24 Aug, 10:00AM till done

    TBC

    Nominees are required to give a speech and answer questions from the audience during the Campaign Speech. This event will serve as a platform for the school to better understand your direction or purpose during your office period. All undergraduates are welcomed to join in and ask any candidate questions during the candidate's Q&A. The whole session will be live streamed and you can also ask questions online.

  • 8

    Voting Period

    26 Aug and 27 Aug, 12:00PM to 05:00PM

    Student Lounge

    Nominees are elected into the 22nd Management Committee only if at least 2/3 of their votes are in support of their candidacy. All Computing undergraduates may cast their votes. Voting guide and instructions will be released closer to the date.


  • 9

    Announcement of Voting Results

    27 Aug, 08:00PM

    The General Elections voting results will be released via the elections website at the given time. Successful nominees will be elected into the 22nd MC and enter the Internal Elections to decide on the respective positions.

  • 10

    Internal Election

    28 Aug and 29 Aug, 06:00PM to 10:00PM

    The newly elected members of the MC will attempt to fill up the positions specified in the Constitution over the 2 days. The Internal Elections is not open to the public, but will be streamed online for the public's view.
