About

Join the Team

Have you participated in previous Computing Club activities, and experienced first hand the great synergy running through the organisers, as well as with the participants? Do you see great experience, potential and passion in yourself, which can be put into use to further the cause of your fellow faculty-mates?

As students of SoC, we have reaped much benefits from the efforts of the previous Computing Club Management Committees. Heed the calling to contribute, to serve and to lead. Step up, present yourself to your fellow students, and take up the challenge as the NUS Students' Computing Club 22nd Management Committee.

22nd MC General Elections

This is an overview of the 22nd MC General Elections process, along with important dates.

  • 1

    22nd MC Information Sessions

    13 Aug and 14 Aug, TBC

    TBC

    Information Session is designed to help potential candidates understand what the Management Committee does. The 21st MC will be there to share their past experiences and answer any queries during the session.

  • 2

    20th Annual General Meeting

    TBC

    TBC

    The Annual General Meeting is an annual event where 21st Management Committee position holders will be sharing what they had done during their term. Students who are interested in running the 22nd Management Committee are strongly encouraged to attend the AGM to better understand the positions that they are running for.

  • 3

    Nomination Period

    13 Aug to 17 Aug, TBC

    Nominations period is the time for club members to sign up and step up to be a nominee in the upcoming elections. You need to submit documents, which will be uploaded nearer to the elections.

    Only full-time matriculated undergraduates of the School of Computing are eligible to stand for election, propose or second a candidate, and vote at the ballots.

  • 4

    Interview with advisors

    19 Aug

    This session is for our faculty advisors to get to know the nominees better. Nominees will be informed of their individual interview details via email.

  • 5

    Appeal Period

    18 Aug to 20 Aug, TBC

    Any Club Member whose nomination is rejected by the Election Committee shall have the right to a single appeal to the Election Committee. The Election Committee's decision following an appeal is final thereafter. The appeal must be submitted, in writing, to an Election Committee member.

  • 6

    Campaigning Period

    22 Aug to 23 Aug

    Successful nominees will be allowed to campaign and garner votes for the position they are running for. Nominees are encouraged to put up posters on the noticeboard around School of Computing, and engage the students in school.

    Nominees will have to adhere to the rules as indicated on our Election Standing Order, NUS Student Conduct as well as instructions from the Elections Committee.

  • 7

    22nd MC Elections Campaign Speech

    24 Aug, TBC

    25 Aug, TBC

    TBC

    Nominees are required to give a speech and answer questions from the audience during the Campaign Speech. This event will serve as a platform for the school to better understand your direction or purpose during your office period.

  • 8

    Voting Period

    26 Aug and 27 Aug, TBC

    TBC

    Nominees are elected into the 22nd Management Committee only if at least 2/3 of their votes are in support of their candidacy. All Computing undergraduates may cast their votes. Voting guide and instructions will be released closer to the date.


  • 9

    Annoucement of Voting Results

    28 Aug, TBC

    Our Election Returning Officer, Mr. Lim Wai Lun, will announce the voting results at a stipulated timing released closer to the date. Successful nominees will be elected into the 22nd MC and enter the Internal Elections to decide on the respective positions.

  • 10

    Only full-time matriculated undergraduates of the School of Computing are eligible to stand for election, propose or second a candidate, and vote at the ballots.

  • 11
