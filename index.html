About

Join the Team

Have you participated in previous Computing Club activities, and experienced first hand the great synergy running through the organisers, as well as with the participants? Do you see great experience, potential and passion in yourself, which can be put into use to further the cause of your fellow faculty-mates?

As students of SoC, we have reaped much benefits from the efforts of the previous Computing Club Management Committees. Heed the calling to contribute, to serve and to lead. Step up, present yourself to your fellow students, and take up the challenge as the NUS Students' Computing Club 21st Management Committee.

Did you know? The Management Committee is officially part of the SoC Student Leadership Programme. Joining the Management Committee accords you service hours under the programme.

The 21st MC General Elections has yet to begin. For our activities, please refer to our club's home page.

Timeline

21st MC General Elections

This is an overview of the 21st MC General Elections process, along with important dates.

  • 1

    21st MC Information Sessions

    13 Aug and 14 Aug, 12 PM to 4 PM

    Cerebro

    Information Session is designed to help potential candidates understand what the Management Committee does. 20th MC will share their past experiences during the information session.

  • 2

    20th Annual General Meeting

    14 Aug, 6 PM to 11 PM

    SR1

    The Annual General Meeting is an annual event where 20th Management Committee position holders will be sharing their review of the past year. Students who are interested in running the 21st Management Committee are strongly encouraged to attend the AGM to better understand the positions that they are running for.

  • 3

    Nomination Period

    13 Aug to 15 Aug, till 6 PM

    Nominations period is the time for club members to sign up and step up to be a nominee in the upcoming elections. Nominations are open from 13 August 12pm till 15 August 6pm. You may submit your nomination forms (FORM A & FORM B) via email elections@nuscomputing.com.

    Only full-time matriculated undergraduates of the School of Computing are eligible to stand for election, propose or second a candidate, and vote at the ballots.

  • 4

    Interview with advisors

    16 Aug

    This session is for our faculty advisors to get to know the nominees better. Nominees will be informed of their individual interview details via email.

  • 5

    Appeal Day

    17 Aug, till 6 PM

    Any Club Member whose nomination is rejected by the Election Committee shall have the right to a single appeal to the Election Committee. The Election Committee's decision following an appeal is final thereafter. The appeal must be submitted, in writing, to an Election Committee member.

  • 6

    Campaigning Period

    20 Aug to 22 Aug

    Successful nominees will be allowed to campaign and garner votes for the position they are running for. Nominees are encouraged to put up posters on the noticeboard around School of Computing, and engage the students in school.

    Nominees will have to adhere to the rules as indicated on our Election Standing Order, NUS Student Conduct as well as instructions from the Elections Committee.

  • 7

    21st MC Elections Campaign Speech

    21 Aug, 6 PM to 11 PM

    22 Aug, 10 AM to 10 PM

    SR1

    Nominees are required to give speech and answer questions from the audience during the Campaign Speech. This event will serve as a platform for the school to better understand your direction or purpose during your office period.

  • 8

    Voting Period

    23 Aug and 24 Aug, 12 PM to 5 PM

    Student Lounge

    Nominees are elected into the 21st Management Committee only if at least 2/3 of their votes are in support of their candidacy. All Computing undergraduates may cast their votes in the Student Lounge from 12pm to 5pm.

  • 9

    Annoucement of Voting Results

    25 Aug, 3 PM

    Our Election Returning Officer, Mr. Joel Sim, will announce the voting results at about 3 PM. Successful nominees will be elected into the 21st MC and enter the Internal Elections to fill up the position.

  • 10

    Annoucement of Voting Results

    27 Aug and 28 Aug, 6 PM to 11 PM

    The newly elected members of the MC will attempt to fill up the positions specified in the Constitution over the 2 days, should there be no by-election.
